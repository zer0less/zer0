Sorry Letter

Dear Friends,

My senior year and summer were by far the best moments I lived. The best 18 year old birthday presents I could ask for. I wish I could have enjoyed it more. You were all so nice to me even though I never returned anything. But my thoughts kept me from enjoying it with you all. I know that hurt some of you. But I also hurt myself in the process. My health took a hit for the worse and my depression grew each day. My thoughts were corrupted with dangerous information.

I never thought about changing. I kept doing the same thing everyday. I tried to escape the reality I was living in by doing stuff including some that I regret. I talked with strangers online. I watched videos, shows, and small vids until my brain was fried. Everyday I was tired and the sun hurt my already dry eyes.

But I must thank you all. Without you all (and her), I don't think I would still be here today. My mind would have taken control. I used to think of doing it almost each day. My mind was viewing each way I could do it. One day, I tried to do it by drowning myself. The salty tears mixed with the water. My words were cut with the water. I was breating water. My lungs were screaming for air. What am I doing? I don't want to quit like this. The survival instinct of my body took control and brought me back up.

But now, I want to change. Now, I want to be happier. Now, I want to see the world in colors. Now, I want to see the beautiful nature. Now, I want to see it all. Now, I want to be strong. Now, I don't want to be always tired. Now, I don't want to be depressed. Now, I don't want to lie to everyone. Now, I want to live.

I want to take back the time I lost with you all. I didn't get to know you all like I wanted to. I want to hang out with you all. I want to laugh with you all. I want to eat with you all. I want to enjoy life with you all. I am always up for anything so hit me up.

I am glad I was able to meet you all. You truly are the best friends in the world.

Sincerly,
